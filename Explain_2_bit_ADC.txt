First I have taken a sine wave with DC component 2.5 and with amplitude 2.5 and with a frequency equals to 1.
Then they given that we can use only two voltage sources,So one of them will be this sin signal. And the other will be a constant 5V.
Now as our aim is to divide that 0-5 amplitude sin signal into 4 bins (since for 2 bits resolution 4 parts will be possible).So, length of each bin would be 5/4=1.25.
Now our comparator voltages would be 1.25,2.5.3.75 so I used 3 op-amps.I have set op-amps in that way that it will be give the output as  0 if the signal voltage is less than the comparator's voltage.Else it will give 1.
So then at the last we will get outputs like,000,100,110,111
Now I had converted them into 2-bits so that we will get what we required (2-bit resolution).
After that,I got the simplified boolean expression for the MSB and LSB bits.
Then I used Logic gates in LTspice to get them from the 3-bit answer.
After getting them, I had plotted the graph of sin signal,MSB signal,LSB signal, and the ((2*MSB)+LSB)*1.25 to get the potential square type of signal in a 2-bit resolution.
